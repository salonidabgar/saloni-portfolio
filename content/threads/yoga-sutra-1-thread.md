# Twitter/X Thread — Yoga Sutra 1.1 ("atha yogānuśāsanam")

Companion to blog post: /blog/yoga-sutra-1-atha-yoganushasanam
5 tweets · contemplative voice · credit to Adhyatma Yoga in final tweet

---

**1/**
The first Yoga Sutra is just three words.

अथ योगानुशासनम् — *atha yogānuśāsanam.*
"Now begins the discipline of yoga."

I got stuck on the first word. Not *yoga*. Not *discipline*.

*Atha.* Now.

---

**2/**
Why "now"?

Because before this, you were living recklessly — reacting to everything, aware of nothing.

Yoga doesn't ask you to stay that person.

It asks you to become the other version of yourself, the one waiting underneath.

---

**3/**
My reading of these sutras will keep changing.

Like water where the sand has finally settled, and you can suddenly see the fishes.

What I can't see today, a clearer chitta will read tomorrow.

---

**4/**
Hearing it (shravan) is step one.

Then manan — turning it over until you can *see* it.

Then nidhidhyasan — living it. In traffic. At your desk. Mid-argument.

The knowing has to become the doing.

---

**5/**
Where I start: witness your thoughts. Before reacting, pause and ask what's causing it. The path opens there.

Not someday. Not when I'm ready. Now.
